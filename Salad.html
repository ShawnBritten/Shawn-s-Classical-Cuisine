  1. In a stockpot, render the bacon by cooking it slowly and allowing it to release its fat.
  2. sweat the celery, carrots, onion, and garlic in the fat without browning them.
  3. Add the stock, peas, and marjoram Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer and cook until the peas are soft, approximately 1–1½ hours.
  4. (Optional) - Purée the soup by passing it through a food mill or by using a blender, food processor or vertical cutter/mixer (VCM).
  5. Return the soup to the stockpot.
  6. A great flavor and color enhancer to your soup would be adding a ½ C of frozen peas.
  7. Blend it into the soup using an immersion blender.
  8. Bring the soup to a simmer and, if necessary, adjust the consistency by adding hot stock. Adjust the seasonings and serve, garnished with croutons.

Salad Niçoise is a classical salad.

I like to deconstruct the salads garnish. Bring out the best in each garnish by cooking or designing each one. Then reconstructing the salad. Its name comes from the olive Niçoise. To me it is a combination of vegetables in a salad with sweet’s and sours coming from the vinaigrette and pickled garnish. I like pickling a lot of the garnish before hand to highlight nuances. This also reminds me of Ernest Hemingway’s book “The Sun also Rises” where tuna in Italy was served differently than what we are used to.

  1. Make the vinaigrette dressing by whisking all ingredients together in a bowl.
  2. Simmer the red bliss potatoes in salted water with a touch of white wine vinegar until tender. This brings out the red in the red bliss.
  3. Trim the green beans and blanch in boiling salted water.
  4. Start the raw shell on egg in cold water. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Once simmering time for 10 minutes then put egg under cold running water. While the water is running peel the egg and cut in quarters just before serving salad.
  6. Throw some of the vinaigrette into the potato, tomato, onion, well before service to allow them to pickle slightly.
  7. Line each cold plate with two romaine lettuce leaves and/or three mixed lettuce leaves. Arrange the remaining ingredients attractively. Use the contrasting shapes, colors and textures to create an attractive presentation.
  8. At service time, whisk the dressing to combine the ingredients and pour approximately 2 fluid ounces (60 milliliters) over each salad.

Most Niçoise salads serve cucumbers and artichokes not pictured. The recipe also has used a basic vinaigrette instead of the Dijon lemon one we chose.
