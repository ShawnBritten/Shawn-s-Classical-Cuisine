  1. Place egg yolks, water, and vinegar in a medium stainless steel bowl.
  2. Fill a double boiler with about 2 inches of water, or pot large enough to have the bowl sit on top without touching the water. Bring the water to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Place the bowl on top of the pot, whisk the egg mixture vigorously and continuously until the eggs turn cream colored and thickened, about 3 minutes.
  4. Remove the bowl from the pot. Whisk in the lemon juice to help the egg stop cooking.
  5. Melt the butter in a skillet, it should be between 140 to 145oF (60 to 63oC) when adding to the egg yolk mixture.
  6. Place the bowl containing the egg mixture nested in a kitchen towel to help keep the bowl in place when whisking in the butter.
  7. Very slowly start to add a few drops of melted butter at a time into the eggs, whisking constantly to create an emulsion.
  8. Continue to gradually add in the butter, constantly whisking until all of the butter is incorporated. The sauce should be thick and velvety.
  9. Add in salt and cayenne pepper to taste. Add more lemon juice if desired for a more tartness.

Poached Eggs (Eggs Benedict)

2 Eggs Poached

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, then reduce to low (or turn off the heat).
  2. Crack the egg in a fine mesh sieve (over a small bowl) and remove the liquidy part of whites.
  3. Transfer the egg to a small ramekin or bowl.
  4. Add one tablespoon of light-colored vinegar to the pot and stir to create a vortex.
  5. Pour the ramekin with the egg into the middle of the vortex and set a timer for 3 minutes.
  6. Once the egg is done, use a slotted spoon to remove the poached egg. Dab with a paper towel to remove excess water and eat immediately.

Eggs Benedict

  1. toast an English muffin or toast.
  2. Stack a tomato slice and one slice of bacon on each muffin halve.
  3. Lay one, just poached egg, on each muffin stack and spoon hollandaise over each egg.
  4. Sprinkle with paprika and herbs.
