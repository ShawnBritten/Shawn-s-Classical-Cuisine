  1. Heat a heavy pot until very hot and add the oil.
  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper, dust with flour, and add it to the pot, browning it well on all sides. Do not overcrowd the pot. If necessary, cook the beef in several batches.
  3. Remove the seared beef.
  4. Add the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic and sauté until the onions are slightly browned. Add the Thyme.
  5. Add the wine and stock slowly, stirring to prevent lumps.
  6. Add the beef back into the pot and simmer till the beef is tender. If you go to fast you may need to add more stock. If you go to slow you may need to raise the heat.
  7. Once the beef is tender add tomato and peas for color
  8. Salt and Pepper to taste. Optional butter to make the stew glisten.
  9. Degrease the stew by skimming off the fat. Serve

  1. Bring the cooking liquid (usually a stock) to a simmer.
  2. Heat the fat in a separate heavy saucepan over medium low heat.
  3. Add any onions, garlic, mushrooms, or other flavoring ingredients and sweat for 1–2 minutes without browning. Remove mushrooms (it is ok if some onions get taken out as long as all the mushrooms are removed.)
  4. Begin to add wine and then the simmering stock, 2 fluid ounces (120 milliliters) at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until each portion of cooking liquid is almost fully absorbed before adding the next.
  5. Test for doneness after the grains have cooked for approximately 18–20 minutes.
  6. Add cream and slightly cook out.
  7. Remove from heat and stir in butter, grated cheese, herbs or other flavoring ingredients as directed. Garnish and serve immediately.
