Grilling and Fettuccini Alfredo with Homemade Pasta

Whether it is scallops, shrimp, or chicken, grilling is a direct heat method that should add flavor to your protein through the searing or charring of the surface of the meat. It is important to create a surface on the meat or seafood that when done properly, will let go of the pan when it is ready. With seafood, it generally takes seconds, not minutes. Remember to always season your protein first before grilling.

Shrimp- They have an automatic temperature done thermometer installed and when they turn pink, they are done generally in seconds. Any longer, and they will turn to rubber.

Scallops- They have a tendon that needs to be removed if it hasn’t already been done. Scallops will take just a little longer than shrimp, and it is good to not move them once they have been placed in a medium hot pan. Once you flip the scallop, you can baste the scallop to completion by using the butter or pan juices to do the job.

Chicken will take longer depending on the thickness. Grill marks on one side, flip and finish on the other side, basting with pan juices to completion.

Serve immediately! Which means, your pasta and sauce should be close to completion.

Today, we will be making homemade pasta in a fettuccini Alfredo sauce, topped with tomato Provençale.

Fresh Pasta

  1. Mound the flour on a workbench.
  2. Make a well in the center. Place the eggs in the well and whip them with a fork.
  3. Use your fingers to stir the eggs with olive oil and salt, gradually bringing more flour into the center.
  4. Use a dough scraper, to add more flour to the egg mixture, stirring constantly until a firm dough is formed.

Cooking the Pasta

  1. To cook the pasta, bring a pot of salted water to boil. Add pasta and reduce heat slightly.
  2. in about 2-3 minutes pull a piece of pasta out and taste it.
  3. If it is still tough keep it in the water.
  4. If soft strain, add a touch of olive oil, salt and pepper to the strained pasta and toss with the sauce.

  2. In a separate pot, make a white roux with the flour and butter.
