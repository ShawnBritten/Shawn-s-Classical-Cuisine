Chausser Chicken Stir-fried Vegetable

Hunter’s Chicken. (Chausser Chicken) with Stir-Fried Vegetable

Chausser Sauce

This is the Sauce recipe from the “Cooking with Mickey Around the World Cookbook”

WDC 1986. This recipe was the one that was used for the Tavern Chicken that I experienced at my first location, the Liberty Tree Tavern. Here, the Brown Sauce is made, and the Vegetable Sauce is added to it.

Brown Sauce

Item Quantity Preparation Price
Whole Butter 1 tbl 1.69
All-Purpose Flour 1 tbl 2.19
Whole Garlic Clove 1 ea crushed 0.46
Pepper 1/16 tsp 1.00
Salt to taste 0.79
Tomato Sauce 1 tbl 3.99
Chicken Stock 1 cup 3.65
Total: $13.77
  1. Heat butter in a saucepan. Stir in flour. Cook and stir for 5 minutes.
  2. Add stock gradually to flour, stirring constantly with a whisk until mixture is thickened and smooth.
  3. Add pepper and garlic and simmer 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add vegetable sauce and cook 5 minutes longer. Add salt, if needed.

  2. Add mustard and catsup. Add sauces together. (Add to sauce above)

Break down chicken breasts as instructed from a whole chicken.

Stir-Fried Vegetable

combination of vegetables: Broccoli, carrots, and grean beans

  1. In a large sauté pan, heat and add olive oil and immediately add more dense longer to cook vegetables first.
  2. Sauté for 2-3 minutes before adding quicker to cook vegetables to the pan.
  3. Continue cooking until vegetables still have texture, (Al Dente). Add one clove crushed garlic at the end and season to taste.
  4. Butter and a squeeze of lemon is good here as well.
